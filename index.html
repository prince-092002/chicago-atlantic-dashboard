🗄️ SQL Data Extraction Queries

Below are the SQL queries used to extract and transform data from the 8 source datasets into the dashboard visualizations. These demonstrate structured analytical thinking applied to real Chicago Atlantic financial data.

1. REFI Geographic Portfolio Concentration

Source: REFI_GEOGRAPHY.csv — Extracts state-level loan principal distribution across 18 jurisdictions.

-- Query: REFI portfolio geographic breakdown by state -- Purpose: Identify largest state exposures and limited-license state concentration SELECT Jurisdiction, Outstanding_Principal, Portfolio_Pct, SUM(Outstanding_Principal) OVER (ORDER BY Outstanding_Principal DESC ROWS BETWEEN UNBOUNDED PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW) AS Cumulative_Principal, ROUND(SUM(Portfolio_Pct) OVER (ORDER BY Outstanding_Principal DESC ROWS BETWEEN UNBOUNDED PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW), 1) AS Cumulative_Pct FROM refi_geography WHERE Jurisdiction != 'Total' ORDER BY Outstanding_Principal DESC;

2. REFI Credit Risk Rating Distribution by Vintage

Source: REFI_RISK_RATING.csv — Aggregates loan principal by internal risk rating (1-5) and origination year.

-- Query: Risk rating distribution with vintage year breakdown SELECT Risk_Rating, COALESCE(Y2025, 0) AS Vintage_2025, COALESCE(Y2024, 0) AS Vintage_2024, COALESCE(Y2023, 0) AS Vintage_2023, COALESCE(Y2022, 0) AS Vintage_2022, COALESCE(Y2021, 0) AS Vintage_2021, Total, ROUND(Total * 100.0 / SUM(Total) OVER(), 1) AS Pct_of_Portfolio FROM refi_risk_rating WHERE Risk_Rating != 'Total' AND Total > 0 ORDER BY Risk_Rating;

3. Illinois Cannabis Monthly Retail Sales Trend

Source: Retail_Sales_data.csv — Monthly medical + adult-use revenue from Nov 2015 to Dec 2025.

-- Query: Monthly cannabis sales by category (2020-2024 focus period) SELECT Month_Year, SUM(CASE WHEN Measure_Name = 'Medical Sales' THEN Measure_Value ELSE 0 END) AS Medical_Sales, SUM(CASE WHEN Measure_Name = 'Adult Use Sales' THEN Measure_Value ELSE 0 END) AS Adult_Use_Sales, SUM(Measure_Value) AS Total_Sales FROM retail_sales WHERE YEAR(Month_Year) BETWEEN 2020 AND 2024 GROUP BY Month_Year ORDER BY Month_Year;

4. REFI & LIEN Historical Stock Price Performance

Source: HistoricalData REFI.csv HistoricalData LIEN.csv — Daily OHLCV data for both tickers.

-- Query: Monthly closing prices for REFI and LIEN (joined) SELECT r.Trade_Month, r.Close_Price AS REFI_Close, l.Close_Price AS LIEN_Close, r.Monthly_Volume AS REFI_Volume FROM ( SELECT DATE_TRUNC('month', Date) AS Trade_Month, LAST_VALUE(Close_Price) OVER (PARTITION BY DATE_TRUNC('month', Date) ORDER BY Date) AS Close_Price, SUM(Volume) AS Monthly_Volume FROM refi_historical GROUP BY 1 ) r LEFT JOIN ( SELECT DATE_TRUNC('month', Date) AS Trade_Month, LAST_VALUE(Close_Price) OVER (PARTITION BY DATE_TRUNC('month', Date) ORDER BY Date) AS Close_Price FROM lien_historical GROUP BY 1 ) l ON r.Trade_Month = l.Trade_Month ORDER BY r.Trade_Month;

5. Illinois Weekly Retail Sales & Average Pricing

Source: Cannabis_Retail_Sales_by_Week_Ending.csv — Weekly granularity with product counts and average prices.

-- Query: Weekly total sales and average product prices (2024) SELECT Week_Ending, Adult_Use_Retail_Sales, Medical_Retail_Sales, Total_Sales, Adult_Use_Avg_Price, Medical_Avg_Price, Total_Products_Sold, AVG(Total_Sales) OVER (ORDER BY Week_Ending ROWS BETWEEN 3 PRECEDING AND CURRENT ROW) AS Rolling_4Wk_Avg FROM cannabis_weekly_sales WHERE YEAR(Week_Ending) = 2024 ORDER BY Week_Ending;

6. Adult-Use Price Per Gram Trend Analysis

Source: Adult_Use_Prices_data.csv — Monthly average prices by product category (Buds, Edibles, Concentrates, etc.).

-- Query: Average price per gram by category over time SELECT Month_Year, Product_Category, Avg_Price_Per_Gram, LAG(Avg_Price_Per_Gram, 12) OVER (PARTITION BY Product_Category ORDER BY Month_Year) AS Price_YoY_Ago, ROUND((Avg_Price_Per_Gram - LAG(Avg_Price_Per_Gram, 12) OVER ( PARTITION BY Product_Category ORDER BY Month_Year)) / NULLIF(LAG(Avg_Price_Per_Gram, 12) OVER ( PARTITION BY Product_Category ORDER BY Month_Year), 0) * 100, 1) AS YoY_Change_Pct FROM adult_use_prices WHERE Product_Category = 'Buds' ORDER BY Month_Year;

📋 Research Methodology & Documentation

This document outlines the analytical methodology, data sources, and research steps undertaken to produce this Chicago Atlantic performance dashboard — demonstrating data analyst skills and deep company understanding.

Research Objective

Quantify Chicago Atlantic's business thesis that regulated-state cannabis lending delivers attractive risk-adjusted returns, using publicly available financial data, SEC filings, and Illinois cannabis market datasets. Position this analysis as a post-interview follow-up demonstrating:

  • Proficiency in financial data analysis and data visualization
  • Understanding of Chicago Atlantic's dual-vehicle strategy (REFI mREIT + LIEN BDC)
  • Ability to synthesize quantitative data with qualitative market insights
  • SQL querying and data transformation skills
1Data Collection & Source Identification
Identified and collected 8 primary datasets spanning financial, market, and pricing data:
REFI GEOGRAPHY.csv Portfolio geographic breakdown (18 states, $400M)
REFI RISK RATING.csv Internal risk ratings by vintage year (2021-2025)

Adult Use Prices_data.csv IL adult-use price per gram by product (2020–2025)
Supplementary: Chicago Atlantic IR releases, SEC Form 10-K/10-Q, earnings call transcripts
2Financial Performance Analysis
REFI (mREIT): Analyzed FY2024 results — $410M total loan principal across 30 companies, net income of $37M ($1.88/share), distributable earnings of $40M ($2.03/share), net interest income of $55M. Weighted average yield to maturity: 17.2%. Dividend of $2.06/share (including $0.18 special) = 16.8% forward yield. Book value declined slightly to $14.83/share.

LIEN (BDC): Analyzed FY2024 results — portfolio FMV of $275M (100% senior secured), total investment income of $21.7M, NII of $9.5M ($0.91/share). NAV/share: $13.20. Completed Loan Portfolio Acquisition in Q4 expanding net assets from $82.5M to $301.2M. Q4 dividend raised 36% to $0.34/share = 13.6% forward yield. Zero non-accruals.
3Geographic Portfolio Analysis
Used REFI GEOGRAPHY.csv to map portfolio concentration across 18 states. Key findings: Illinois leads at 19% ($77.4M), followed by Ohio (16%, $65.1M), Florida (14%, $57.0M), Pennsylvania (9%, $36.3M), and Michigan (8%, $32.1M). Top 5 states represent 66% of total portfolio. ~90% of portfolio deployed in limited-license states — confirming the regulatory moat thesis.
4Credit Quality & Risk Assessment
Analyzed REFI RISK RATING.csv — 48% of portfolio rated "2" (low risk), 42% rated "3" (performing), 10% rated "4" (watch). No loans in rating "5" (non-accrual). Zero non-accruals across both REFI and LIEN. LIEN: 80% floating-rate loans with 99% interest rate floors, secured net leverage of 1.5x at borrower level.
5Illinois Cannabis Market Analysis
Used IL retail sales data to confirm market health: 2024 total sales exceeded $2.0B (+2.5% YoY), 4th consecutive record year, 3rd largest US market. Unit volume grew +13% YoY. Monthly adult-use sales stabilized at $133-153M range. Average price per gram for flower declined from ~$10 (2023) to ~$6 (late 2025), indicating market maturation and competitive pricing while total revenue still grows — a sign of volume growth outpacing price compression.
6Competitive Moat Analysis
Identified three structural moats enabling 17%+ yields: (1) Banking Exclusion Premium: Federal Schedule I classification prevents traditional lenders from serving cannabis operators, creating 800-1000 bps spread premium. (2) Limited-License Scarcity: ~25 states cap operator licenses, producing monopoly-like cash flows. (3) First-Lien Collateral Stack: Senior-secured loans backed by real estate, equipment, IP, and state licenses with conservative 1.5x leverage.
7Dashboard Design & Visualization
Built an interactive HTML dashboard modeled after Power BI using Chart.js for data visualizations. Design choices: dark theme for financial terminal aesthetic, color-coded REFI (blue) vs LIEN (green) metrics, interactive hover effects, gauge-style progress bars for credit quality metrics. All data embedded inline from CSV sources. Dashboard is self-contained in a single HTML file for easy distribution.
